1 Market Participants and Market Structure

Definition 1.1 (issuer) An issuer is a legal entity that sells securities to finance its operations. Examples:

  1. Businesses that need capital to grow.
  2. Governments that borrow funds to pay their bills.

They raise capital through:

  1. Debt securities (bonds, notes, or debt instruments): represents the amount of indebtedness that the issuer owes to the investor. Bond customers are creditors. An issuer defaults if it misses an interest or principal payment.
  2. Equity securities (stocks): represents ownership in the company. Payment of dividends is voluntary.

Financial firms are typically broker-dealers or investment advisors.

Definition 1.2 (broker-dealer) A broker is a person whose business effects agency transactions in securities for the account of others (match buyers and sellers and earn commission). A dealer engages in principal transactions, buying securities from their clients and holding them in inventory.

In short, a broker earns commission, while a dealer earns a spread.
